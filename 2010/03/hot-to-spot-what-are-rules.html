Randy Knaub: What Are the Rules?

March 11, 2010 Written by Randy Knaub Process

As I thought about this month’s theme, it occurred to me that I have never spotted a film or a television show. Well, let me restate that last sentence: If you had asked if I have spotted a film I would have shown something like this —

I think I understand the process of spotting and it seems similar to an artform like origami or animal balloon sculpting. Just kidding of course — but I think I can learn this art form… maybe I just need the practice of taking picture without music and trying to find the appropriate spots music feels appropriate for, and figure it our through trial by error.

So help me, SCOREcasters — Give me YOUR tips on how one could begin to learn this important skill in the toolbox of a composer. How should one go about practicing “spotting”? Where is a good place to start? Is there a particular film that would be the best one to start with? Can you spot without the involvement of a director? What are some myths about “spotting” that are old and stale wives’ tales and need to be hung out in the light of day once and for all?

When I normally write music for anything, it’s based upon a mood I’m feeling or something I see that inspires me to write for the visual mood or setting. Since this is not a “Hey Randy! How do I spot gear” article, I’m looking for you all to help me out this time around.

COMMENTS are now open below…I’m all ears!

(Until next month — when I spot an endangered species!)

_____________________

RANDY KNAUB is an audio engineer, producer, songwriter, keyboardist, and music technologist. He composes music and sound bites at his studio, Jireh Productions, and works with local artists recording demos and CDs as well as pursuing corporate multimedia clients. Randy also consults for and beta tests for numerous music software and hardware manufacturers. His clients range from Martin Marietta, McDonalds, The United Way, Colorado Lottery, Visual Communications Group, and Jeppesen. You can find his SCOREcast bio (and links to his other sites) here.
