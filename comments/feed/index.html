]]> Comment on Forget Tech, It’s About the Notes by Jon Adamic ../../2013/01/14/forget-tech-its-about-the-notes/#comment-2504 Fri, 31 May 2013 16:33:32 +0000 ../../?p=1959#comment-2504 Let me give everyone here a big tip.
Don't use loops or pads. Don't use pre-made string runs or a "full" orchestra patch. Write your own music, note for note. Otherwise, you won't grow and your music will sound bland and generic. You are writing freaking orchestral music, not some two bit rock track.

Using pre-made loops and full ensemble patches is everything that's wrong with writing today. I don't quite understand how people can actually present a piece stating they wrote it, when in fact they did not. If sections are not written by you, but just placed inside the project, you should be ashamed of yourself.

If someone says, make this score into sheet music, and you are not able to, you need to do some self revaluation.

Learn to do what the hell you say you do. You write orchestral music? Then do it, and don't rely on cheap tools to make you look better.

Now, I understand that some may say "we don't have time in such a tight deadline." To be frank, 99% of the time that's total BS, and just denial that helps you sleep at night. If you are working on an actual television show, and need to produce 20 minutes of music a week, then I can start to see why. But if you learn how to orchestrate properly (as no one does these days), writing strings runs and brass textures should take a couple minutes. It would be more original and fit the piece more adequately if you write them yourself.

Once again. If you want to call yourself an orchestral composer, be one. You all know who you are.
