Deane Ogden: It’s March… and We’re All Ears

by Deane Ogden · 4 Comments · in Uncategorized

Welcome to a new month at SCOREcastOnline.com, where we’ll be focusing this time around on the art and skill of “spotting.” Look for some great new articles from our regular contributing editors as well as a few surprise guests as they weigh in on this important aspect of our craft — where music should be, and where music shouldn’t be.

Along with all of that, Deane and Lee are prepping the new podcast episode — #27 — and have several tricks up their sleeve for this installment of the show.

In this episode, in addition to announcing the winner of the SCOREcastOnline.com Theme Song Contest, the guys are opening up the podcast for the first time to Listener Questions. If you have a burning question you’ve always wanted to ask Deane and Lee, either as veteran composers or as the head cheeses around here, now is the time to ask… and they will read it on the air and respond.

You can ask them anything — “What is the first step I should take in my professional career?”; “How do I get an agent?”; “What should my minimum price be for a minute of music?”; “Which is better, Logic or DP?” — whatever you want to know. You can even ask SCOREcast-related questions if you would like to, things that you’ve been dying to know about the future of the SCO community, the podcast, or even questions about the community as a whole, the proposed union, or anything else that strikes you (as long as it pertains to our business of film music… they boys will not answer for Deane’s love of 80′s hair-metal or Lee’s fascination with yellow BMW’s).

Leave your questions in the COMMENTS section below or in the Shoutbox on the left-hand side of this page. Be sure to leave your name with your question, and what part of the world you are writing in from.

Ask away… we’re all ears!

About The Author

Deane Ogden

SCOREcast's founder and editor-in-chief, DEANE OGDEN, is an American film composer, percussionist, author, and speaker who has scored dozens of feature films including The Sensei, Dreams On Spec, The Way Home, Surrogates, and the Cuba Gooding Jr. actioner The Hit List for Sony Pictures Entertainment. He has also written music for over 600 hours of network television, including the orchestral themes for Michael Phelps’ record-breaking gold medal run during the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games—the most-viewed event in American television history. As a top-call session and live-performance drummer for pop and rock radio, Deane has recorded and performed with numerous artists, including Tina Turner, Joss Stone, Seal, Chris Cornell, David Foster, Kenny Loggins, Gavin DeGraw, Jewel and Rihanna. You can find Deane’s extended bio, blog, and music at his official website, or connect with him personally at Twitter or Facebook.
