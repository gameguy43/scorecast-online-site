James Olszewski: DIY Studio Stocking Stuffers

by SCOREcast Team · 7 Comments · in Uncategorized

Months ago when I saw that December was “Gear Month” I instantly thought “Awesome! Christmas lists!” And so far, even just halfway into the month, there’s been enough gear mentioned that a composer would have to sell a kidney (or 8 or 9 kidneys) to afford it all. Since DIY Studio is all about the bargain-basement, just-getting-started angle, let’s instead think “stocking stuffers”.
Let’s get to it… right after the jump!
I’d like to hear about what you wish you could find in your stocking this Christmas. For the seasoned composers who already have “everything”, we’d love to hear your suggestions on useful bits and bobs we should ask for in our Christmas stockings.

Although I can think of a number of pieces of gear and software I would love to get my hands on, I’ll keep my list simple since we’re talking about stockings.

Native Instruments’ Kore Instruments- These affordable software packs from Native Instruments are great additions to a DIY Studio. They plug into NI’s Kore product, or their free Kore Player software. I’d take any of the titles, but personally I think the North India library is particularly cool.

Korg NanoKey- I’ve mentioned Korg’s Nano line of affordable MIDI controllers before. To me they’re just cool. Portable, solid, and affordable; not many bells and whistles, but a great addition for a road warrior rig, as a second keyboard, or as a control surface to trigger MIDI controllers. And it fits in a stocking.

Good multipurpose mic- I would have to let Santa make the call on this one since I don’t know mics well enough to decide which I would like. I’d have to tell him it would be used to record solo wind, string, and guitar musicians, as well as vocals. Hopefully he’d be able to hook me up. Thoughts from SCOREcasters also appreciated though….

Portable sound recorder- One of these solid-state portable recorders like a Tascam DR-100 or similar would be one of my favorite toys to take on-location to record musicians, samples, or to hum new ideas for tunes into while I’m stuck in traffic.

What else should we have in our stockings? I’m looking for ideas!
