]]> By: Adrian Ellis ../#comment-1525 Fri, 19 Nov 2010 21:20:00 +0000 ../../../../../?p=961#comment-1525 Great thought starter! I think there are two ways to look at it, from our perspective as media composers:

1. Artistically
It should matter. We should be creating unique work that has integrity. We should strive to find and develop our voice. The process and materials are as important to the final product as the composition itself.
OR
It doesn’t matter – you have other priorities, focus. The factory patch works perfectly, why mess about? For that matter, how tired is the use of orchestra in film music? Is that a cliche “patch”?

2. Business-wise
It doesn’t matter. We are paid to deliver a musically based product. As long as it sounds good and works to picture and pleases the client, that’s all that matters.
OR
It does matter, because you are creating a signature, and part of what you are selling is tied in to your unique personal brand. Your process is part of your appeal as a professional. I think this is actually the most salient point here – a way to add value to your projects and your business.

Coincidentally, a project a friend and I are working on right now is a new sound library (with a twist! on the rocks…) yes, time consuming and arduous, but an incredible learning experience, and something which I think will add tremendous value for us down the road.
