April’s Theme: Delivery & Finaling

Posted by SCO Staff on Monday, April 5, 2010 · Leave a Comment 

We’ve covered a lot of ground here at SCOREcastOnline.com this last month as we’ve talked about “spotting films” and we are going to continue to discuss some of the other technical aspects of the post-production process over the next 60 days as we close in on our website’s one-year anniversary in May.
Many of us on the core SCO Editorial Team get a lot of emails and questions about “delivery”. How do we deliver stems, cues, paperwork, contracts, demos, final dub materials, materials for mixing, materials for mastering, etc.? In the whirlwind that is The Final, how does one keep track of sessions, cue numbers, file versions, takes, tracking lists, schedules, and the like? Further down the process, when all is said and done, the score is delivered, and the client is happy, how do you follow up with final invoicing? Who does the cue sheets for the PROs? Who makes sure that the people you’ve sub-contracted out to (orchestrators, copyists, studio rentals, engineers, mixers, equipment cartage companies, etc.) get paid?

This month at SCOREcast, we will attempt to answer all of these questions, and more. We’ll be looking at what happens between the time you finish writing your final notes of music and the time you leave for the tropical getaway you’ve been dreaming of since your director first said six weeks ago, “That’s not really working for me!”
As always, we’ll have some special guests popping in to say hello as well as articles from your favorite SCO editorial staffers who are all working professionals from within the post-production industry.
COMMENTS are always desired and encouraged, as the community doesn’t work without YOUR VOICE—so login below ANY post and say hello. Better yet, offer YOUR advice and methods of madness… the more we all share with and learn from each other the more beneficial the SCO community is the the Post community as a whole.
Have a great month, and as always, thank you for making SCO a part of your daily read!
— The SCOREcastOnline.com Team

The SCOREcast Editorial Staff combines the talents, knowledge, and experience of dozens of top composers working today in film, television, video games, and new media. To learn more about who contributes to SCOREcast, please visit our contibutor page.
