Deane Ogden: When We All Have the Same Toys

May 26th, 2009 | Category: Articles, Business, Gear, Technical

Here’s my question today: Everybody does it, but few will admit it. In Fred Karlin’s must-have book “On the Track” (a book that many consider to be the Bible of film composing), Jerry Goldsmith is quoted as saying that he was “never embarrassed to use a factory preset”. Earlier this week, SCOREcast contributor James Olszewski pointed out to me that over on the SoundsOnline.com website, Danny Elfman mentions that he used StormDrum 2 extensively on his new “Terminator Salvation” score. In several network television shows that are currently airing, you will hear many Spectrasonics Omnisphere patches morphing and wafting their way through the sonic fabric.

In circles of composers, if I had a nickel for every time I heard one say “I design all my own sounds” or “I never use factory sounds”, I’d be a frickin’ millionaire. Now… I know they’re lying. How do I know? I’M A COMPOSER! I know the value of not having to reinvent the wheel on every single project you take on. With abbreviated schedules being what they are nowadays, who has the time?

Maybe even more than “patches” or “presets”, let’s talk about sounds in general. For example, I’ve often wondered when the Waterphone (sometimes called “The Matrix sound”) is going to go out of vogue. The Waterphone is such a unique sound that it is sure to run its course sooner or later, but it never seems to. When you need that weird creepy effect, the Waterphone is the go-to instrument to get it.

That being said, what sounds do you feel get overused? What sounds do you think are now dated and need to be retired? What priority do you place on original sound design, and how do you feel about organic sound design versus ready-made presets from top sound designers in the form of packaged collections?
