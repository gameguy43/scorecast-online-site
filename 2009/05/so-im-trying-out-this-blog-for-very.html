Lee Sanders: To Flit or Not to Flit?

by Lee Sanders · No Comments · in Business

Hi, folks—I’m trying out this blog for the very first time today, and I thought I’d bring up something for discussion—juggling.

I have a bunch of projects going just now (too few of them paying… sigh), and I find that the most fun way to work isn’t necessarily the most efficient way—bouncing back and forth between sequences and shows, writing a bit here and a bit there until I’m stumped (or bored, or sleepy) and then switching gears.

Composing this way definitely takes the edge off of grinding through a particular tedious cue, but I suspect I’m never quite achieving the focus necessary to do my best work.

So what do you think, SCOREcasters? Is it better to power through one thing at a time, or is flitting around (I hesitate to call it multitasking) something we can call a SCOREcast-approved work strategy? Hop into the mix here and let me know what you think.
