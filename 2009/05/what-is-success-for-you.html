Jim Well: What is Success — For You?

by SCOREcast Team · 2 Comments · in Business

You see what film composers do. “That’s what I want!”, you say. But how to begin?

If this resonates for you, start by trying to figure out how you’ll measure success. What is it that you really want? And what’s good enough to keep you happy?

I’ll go first.

Ahem … Let it be known that I, unlike many of my SCOREcast colleagues, am not already successful in this business (yet!). And I’m still working on answering the above questions for myself. So you’re watching my career unfold (very slowly), in real time! Your results may differ …

In my own case – and specifically in the context of creating music for film and other media – I think success will have three components:

(1) being in business for myself, meaningfully satisfying real needs of real customers;

(2) earning enough with my business to enable me to keep doing it – at least most of the time;

(3) working in a way that’s consistent with my chosen car-free, low-travel lifestyle – and living where I live now (which ain’t in LA).

Maybe you’re thinking, that’s pretty broad – except maybe #3. Hm. It doesn’t say anything like, “Scoring for Hollywood blockbusters.”

That would be cool! If that’s what you really want and need, put it in your list, and I’ll watch you! But I don’t need to be in the big time as much as I need (1), (2), and (3) – so for now, I’ll stick with what I’ve written.

Your turn.
