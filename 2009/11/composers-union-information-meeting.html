Deane Ogden: Composers Union Information Meeting · TONIGHT

by SCOREcast Team · No Comments · in Uncategorized

As we’ve been covering here at SCOREcast for the last several weeks, tonight is going to be an interesting night. I sincerely hope to see many of you TONIGHT for a meeting that could very well prove to be the early beginnings of a union for film music composers.

If you are unable to make the meeting, keep your browser pointed here at SCOREcastOnline.com. We will be setting up a special section tonight where you’ll be able to get all of your unionization news in one spot.

The Readers Digest condensed version of what’s happening is that the newly formed Association of Media Composers and Lyricists (AMCL), led by composers Alf Clausen, Bruce Broughton, Jim DiPasquale, and Alan Elliot, have announced that they have been researching the possibility of affiliating with the Teamsters union with the goal of creating a collective bargaining agreement that would organize film composers under a union.

To read my article which followed the initial announcement from a few weeks ago, click here.

I’ve received too many emails and private Facebook messages to count asking me to articulate my feelings about composer unionization on SCOREcastOnline.com, but I feel that it would be premature for me to do so at this time. We just simply don’t know enough yet. That being said, I have a ton of questions that I want answers to tonight at the information meeting in Burbank, and I hope that you do too.

No matter what your feelings are on this issue, there are too many pros and cons to the argument of composer unionization for you to not be asking questions. Regardless of the outcome, you can be certain that your business as a film composer WILL be affected one way or another, pending this effort.

That is why we are urging everyone in our composing community to attend the information meeting being held TONIGHT in Burbank at the Pickwick Gardens Conference Center. If you are in the Los Angeles area, Lee and I will both be in attendance, along with several of our regular SCOREcast contributors, and we’d love to hook up with you there.

Composers Unionization Informational Meeting
Monday, November 16, 2009 · 7:30pm

Pickwick Gardens Conference Center
1001 W Riverside Dr
Burbank, CA 91506-3212

(818) 845-5300

One thing that I feel I must say is that I have a deep respect for the men who are organizing this meeting, and I am impressed by their desire to foster a community discussion that puts this issue through its paces.

No matter what, it’s sure to be an interesting night. See you there!
