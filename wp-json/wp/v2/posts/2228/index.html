Output<\/a> first came on the sample library scene nearly a year and a half ago with their groundbreaking and highly acclaimed release\u00a0REV (SCOREcast review here<\/a>). The library was so forward thinking with such an unorthodox concept that many have wondered how they could possibly follow up that release.<\/span><\/p>\n

The answer comes in the form of their brand new pulse-driven sample library: SIGNAL<\/b>.<\/span><\/p>\n

SIGNAL\u00a0<\/b>combines the warmth of analog and aggressive synthesis with an immense collection of organic, sampled instruments, but the true lure of the library rests with the inclusion of its \u201cPulse Engine\u201d, which allows for up to four independent, tempo-locking rhythms across two separate sound source layers to create that \u201cheartbeat\u201d at the center of your track.<\/span><\/p>\n

We will take a detailed look at the library from the ground up to see what\u2019s included, how it operates and ultimately what it will\u00a0add to your existing sample library arsenal.<\/span><\/p>\n

General Overview<\/h2>\n
\n
\n
\n

SIGNAL\u00a0<\/b>requires Kontakt 5.3.1 or higher and is compatible with the Kontakt Free Player. The library is immense, weighing in at around 40GB. Output utilizes the Continuata Connect Download Utility<\/a> and I personally had a smooth and swift download of the content.\u00a0 <\/span>The library will need to be activated within the Native Instruments Service Center before\u00a0it will become available within the library pane of your Kontakt browser.<\/span><\/p>\n

Before unpacking this beast, let’s look at\u00a0a quick listing of everything included in the library as well as its features:<\/span><\/p>\n

    \n
  • 500 Pulse Instruments for instant playability<\/li>\n
  • 40GB of content, including analog synths, digital synths and organic instruments<\/li>\n
  • Up to four separate pulses at one time<\/li>\n
  • All pulses lock to tempo<\/li>\n
  • Rhythms: Looper, Step Sequencers, Arpeggiators, LFO\u2019s<\/li>\n
  • Four central MACRO sliders unique to each of the 500 Pulse Instruments<\/li>\n
  • Effects can control global parameters and individual layers<\/li>\n
  • Convolution reverb engine<\/li>\n
  • Advanced mode for access to all parameters<\/li>\n
  • Copy and paste settings between layers<\/li>\n
  • Smart tagging preset menu<\/li>\n

    The Preset Browser<\/h2>\n

    Preset Browser<\/p><\/div>\n

    SIGNAL\u00a0<\/b>contains just one patch in the instruments folder by which\u00a0all the contents of the library can be accessed, which I think it is a brilliant layout. The developer recommends (and I agree) that you access the \u201cpreset\u201d browser page first when initially diving into the library. This can be accessed by simply clicking on any\u00a0preset name (e.g. “Dark Clacks”, pictured above) from the main page. The browser is laid out in a familiar, yet very welcomed format; simply click on one (or many) of the \u201csmart tags\u201d in the left-side palette and the right window pane will provide a listing of compatible instruments, based on your choices.<\/span><\/p>\n

    There are five hundred \u2014 That’s right\u2026 500! \u2014\u00a0different instruments in SIGNAL\u00a0<\/b>and <\/b>I couldn\u2019t have asked for a better way to audition\u00a0them than the way this browser is constructed.<\/span><\/p>\n

    So how does it sound on a first play through?\u00a0\u00a0<\/span>I clicked on \u201cEpic\u201d and \u201cOrganic\u201d and chose the patch \u201cSun King\u201d from the browser \u2013 I played a three-note D minor triad and heard a deep, gritty, pulsating arpeggiated figure with two intersecting polyrhythms. Awesome. I proceeded to get lost over the next hour sampling various different instruments from the browser and the immediate attention to the production value and execution of the patches were very impressive.<\/span><\/p>\n
