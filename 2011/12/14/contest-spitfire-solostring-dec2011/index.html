CONTEST: Win Spitfire Audio’s New Solo Strings

By SCO Staff  /   December 14, 2011  /   Comments

It’s SCOREcast’s 2nd Annual Cue Contest—and we are teaming up with Spitfire Audio to give away a copy of SOLO STRINGS! Enter to win today!

Win Spitfire Audio’s Newest Creation

SOLO STRINGS

in SCOREcast’s 2nd Annual Cue Contest

 

This year, SCOREcast is teaming up with Spitfire Audio to give away a copy of their hot new sample library, SOLO STRINGS—the premiere solo string library for Kontakt.

SCOREcast wants to hear what you can do with limited instrumentation, and if you blow our minds, you’ll win your very own copy of SOLO STRINGS by Spitfire! To enter to win, all you need to do is compose a melodic cue that is 60 to 90 seconds long, and uses THREE virtual instruments—one of them being a solo instrument of your choice. So, get your palette together, dream up a memorable melody line, and get to crackin! The contest starts TODAY!

We’ll get to the complete contest rules and eligibility requirements in a second, but right now, here’s the scoop on your prize from Spitfire Audio:

 

SOLO STRINGS by Spitfire

With three legendary players and one legendary studio, Spitfire Audio continues to roll out a definitive set of composers tools with SOLO STRINGS. For this release, Andrew Haveron (violin), Bruce White (viola) and Caroline Dale (cello) were recorded in situ’ the “Spitfire Way” at Lyndhurst Hall, Air Studios—arguably one of the finest in the world and home to hundreds of Hollywood blockbuster films and many top recording artists of our age.

Each instrument features two classes of patch: Legato and Multi Mic.

In Legato mode, three interval types are included: Glissandi, Slur, and Detache—all selectable via velocity, with up to four layers of dynamics selectable via the modulation wheel.

In Multi Mic mode, the various articulations can be dialed in via keyswitches, including long, staccato, spiccato, pizzicato, and harmonics, both long and short. These patches are polyphonic with 3-way mic control, including close, tree and ambient, enabling you to mix and shape your sound to desired levels of room ambience, and route for a true 5.1 experience. Dial just the tree mics in whilst you’re sketching on your laptop for ease on your system, then render back on your “big rig” in full 5.1 before presenting your demos to your director.

SOLO STRINGS by Spitfire features the finest of session skill talents available in the UK today. These are three players you have heard many many times already, whether in concerts around the world, on any recent Hollywood blockbuster recorded in the UK, or a raft of exceptional independent movie appearances and top tracks from a who’s who of pop and rock.

How to Enter the Contest

  1. Meet the Eligibility Requirements (below).
  2. Read and understand the General Conditions and Releases (below).
  3. Compose and produce your entry (cue) based on the Official Rules and Restrictions (below).
  4. Enter your composition in the Contest by sending an email with your NAME (first and last), your MAILING ADDRESS, your AGE, a LIST of plug-in instruments (including sample libraries) used in your entry, the TITLE of your cue entry, and a direct LINK (Soundcloud® links are also permitted) to your cue to at scorecastonline@gmail.com. Limit one entry per person. If multiple entries are received from any person, only the first entry will qualify for the contest.

By participating in this contest, all entrants agree to abide by the official rules and restrictions, eligibility requirements, and general conditions and releases, as outlined below.

Official Rules and Restrictions

Please read through these conditions carefully, and be sure to follow them exactly when composing your cue entry.

  1. Cue must be original and composed by you.
  2. Cue may not contain any pre-recorded melodic elements and can not be based on an existing piece of music.
  3. Cue must ONLY utilize virtual plugin instruments. No live instrumentation allowed.
  4. Cue must be submitted along with an itemized list of plugin instruments used.
  5. Cue must be at least 60 seconds long, but no longer than 90 seconds.
  6. Cue must utilize (3) total virtual instruments, one of which must cover a solo melody (not necessarily a VST dedicated to a solo instrument, but rather a solo instrument of your choice).
  7. Entries will be judged based on the following criteria:
    • Featured use of solo instrument
    • Production value
    • Utilization of limitations (instrument choices and compositional decisions)

Contest Deadline

  1. All entries for SCOREcast’s 2nd Annual Cue Contest must be received on or before December 22, 2011, 5pm Pacific Standard Time.
  2. Winner will be announced December 26, 2011 on SCOREcastOnline.com.

Eligibility Requirements

  1. This Contest is open to people 18 years of age or older. Proof of age may be required.
  2. Employees/contributors of SCOREcast, LLC and its affiliated companies, employees of the prize sponsor (Spitfire Audio) and its affiliates, and members of such employees’ immediate families or households, whether past or present, are ineligible to participate in this Contest.

Winner Notification

  2. Any attempted second entry shall be discarded, and the entrant may be disqualified from the Contest.
  3. Adjudication of entries will be conducted under the supervision of SCOREcastOnline.com and SCOREcast, LLC. The decisions are final and binding in all matters relating to this Contest.
  4. Entries are the property of their respective owners, but will not be returned.
