Open Forum Friday: What Is Your Greatest Motivator?

Sep 17th, 2010 | Category: Articles, Featured

“Motivation” is a strange thing. Be careful how you answer this one: We are certainly aware of the risk involved in even putting this question out there—we are inviting a deluge of the token “writing music is who I am” comments.

If we are honest, though, simply writing is not the primary motivator amongst most who do it everyday. First of all, there are far more easier paths to creative self-realization, and secondly there are far more important things in the overall grand scheme of life itself. After all, if you lost everyone and everything in your life tomorrow, we’d bet money that the least important “treasure” would be all of the shows you’ve worked on, right?

Some are motivated by the joy of “making stuff”, and music just happens to be the conduit by which they are able to do so. Others might be motivated by the fact that they are able to support their family doing such a thing as working in film music, while even others might be driven by the mere fact that they don’t know how to do anything else! Then again, maybe you just like to…oh, I don’t know… EAT.

There are hundreds, if not millions, of potentially motivating factors involved in choosing a career in film music. And so, we ask YOU: What TRULY motivates you to continue on in this tough business? Yeah, yeah, yeah… we get it—you were “made to do this!” :-) But what is it that REALLY keeps you coming back time and time again to have another go, frame after frame, note after note, film after film?

The COMMENTS are open!

  • http://www.nanavant.com/ Nan Avant

    I like this subject of motivation, inspiration which seems to gravitate into gratitude.
    What makes me want to write music for film? Being a composer in independent film, I can’t say it’s the money, so it must be the medium and what I derive from the experience.
    I have composed music without film, solo piano, ensemble music etc but I like having a visual to work with, because it directs me in what I will compose.
    I am motivated when a film has a well written script, good acting and good cinematography. I am motivated by other film composer’s scores and I have always been inspired by themes in film scores. In fact, it’s the themes, motifs in soundtracks that started this whole composing idea within me. I like the creative process, sometimes it’s a challenge to come up with what will work in a film and when its done, this is really what motivates me… the feeling I get when something I have composed is a work that I love to listen to.

  • http://www.brooklyncomposer.com Casual-T

    This, I find, is a very difficult question to answer. Why? Because there are so many things that can be motivational, depending on the circumstances. Sometimes the mere fact of getting paid, is motivation enough, especially if the fridge needs restocking, or the kids have outgrown their shoes (yet again). Other times it might be simply the joy of creating, or the fact that you just listened to some music that inspired you, and you want to see if you can write something so grand/beautiful/interesting/crazy/awe-inspiring/difficult/simple/powerful/etc… All this has been said before, and it seems that many, if not most of us, have somewhat similar thoughts on this. I tried to look for something that went all the way to the core of whatever it is that motivates me to do anything in general, and to write music in particular.

    What I found was that it is incredibly important to me to let my kids know that whatever it is they decide to do with their lives, it should always be in pursuit of their dreams, of what they really want in life. Whether that is to be a plumber running a local business, to conduct the LA Phil at Hollywood Bowl, to be an accountant for a Siberian coal-mining company, or to be a good and generous person who’s sole goal it is to help others. Whatever the dream… Chase it!!!

    Me personally, I don’t see any higher plan or meaning in our existence, no divine forethought, no big final moment of revelation, no grand scheme of things. It’s just us out here, scraping by as good as we can. So other than the pursuit of what fulfills us at the time of our being alive, what makes us happy, what brings us joy… what is there? By following my dreams in life, by showing my kids that you have to adhere to this one rule, I try to teach them what it is that can lead them to a (more or less) happy existence.
