Gateway Scores: What Got You “Hooked”?

June 29, 2009 Written by SCO Staff Craft

As a special SCOREcast theme for this last week of June, you’ll see several posts on the Main Page covering a topic that we all love to talk about: our love of film music.

We asked our SCOREcast Contributors to tell us what film score got them hooked into the idea that scoring films might be the career for them. From many certain classics to some that you might have ever heard of before, it is possible that you’ll be as surprised as we were with the responses.

Have fun reading their stories… and be sure to log in and comment on your own Gateway Score!

  • http://www.mitchellwestmoreland.com Mitchell Westmoreland

    I know I'm not a regular SCOREcast Contributor, but I couldn't resist. This is one of my favorite topics of discussion!

    my thoughts:

    Star Wars (IV) got me hooked (I'm almost positive the fanfare is what made me take up the trumpet years later in school band!)

    The Equalizer, Rumble Fish and the Rhythmatist (Stewart Copeland) made me realize that people can actually make a living as a film composer, even if they were once in one of the biggest rock groups ever.

    Then the 2 films that 'cemented' it:
    The Cook, The Thief, His Wife and her Lover – Michael Nyman
    Twin Peaks; Fire Walk With Me (“The Pink Room”!) – Angelo Badalamenti
    And, just about every David Lynch film's sound design really hits me hard. (Alan Splet mostly responsible :-) )

    –Mitchell

  • http://deaneogden.com Deane Ogden

    Mitchell — I'm embarrassed to say that I didn't discover Badalamenti's score for “Twin Peaks: Fire Walk with Me” until I bought the compilation disc “Pure Moods”.

    That CD has David Byrne's main theme from “The Last Emperor” on it, which is one of my top three favorite pieces of film music. A few tracks later, it also features Angelo's main theme for the Twin Peaks film. Upon hearing that for the first time, I fell madly in love with Badalamenti as well. That first listen started me on the quest to find his score for the film, and subsequently (being the extremist I am about my score collection), the “complete score” by any means possible.

    Angelo Badalamenti's work on that film is the epitome of understatement and restraint. The synth textures he conjures up from cue to cue are absolutely brilliant, and if you line the cues up in chronological order from the film, it is one surreal ride that only Lynch and Badalamenti could deliver.

    Pure. Scary. Genius.

  • http://www.reverbnation.com/wanderlustproject Wanderlust

    I'm not pursuing a career, just composing for pleasure, but what got me hooked into listening to film music was “Chariots Of Fire” and “Blade Runner”, and what motivated me to start exploring music on my own was Bear McCreary's score to the new “Battlestar Galactica” and “Sarah Connor Chronicles”. Before McCreary, all composers I knew were these old, mature guys, and McCreary just turned 30 by the time of “Galactica's” fourth and final season. This convinced me that making music is not only the domain of old guys like Williams and Goldsmith :-).
