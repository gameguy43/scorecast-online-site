Open Forum Friday: New Year’s Eve Edition

December 31, 2010 Written by Jai Meghan Process

2010. The year that was.

Hopefully, you planned well, had a little luck on your side, and made the most of your opportunities, and you are finishing 2010 a little better and brighter than you began it exactly twelve months ago. I know what I did right this year. I also could write another full page on what I did wrong.

But that’s me. Open Forum Fridays are, of course, about you (did you see what I did there?). This year, we all made little steps forward and little steps backward. Such is the ebb and flow of our craft and our industry. We make mistakes; we achieve victory. We forge blindly ahead; we experience unwelcome surprises. Whatever the case, it all works together to build who we become during the next inevitable cycle of writing music for movies, shows, and games.

I Skyped about this today with a few colleagues. As we talked, it became clear that none of us are too keen on making resolutions: We tend to break them about as quickly as we make them. What we are keen on, though, is forward motion. With that in in mind, what did you learn about yourself and your music in 2010 that you will take to heart and move forward with in 2011? What did you do right as a composer living and working in the new climate of the millennium? What did you do wrong that you need to never do again? What worked? What didn’t? What experiment or efficiency will you take with you into 2011? Which failed ones are best left on the other side of 2010?

The COMMENTS are open below.

2010 is dead. 2011 just got a hard smack on the ass. Your move.

  • ChadderMusic

    Hey guys! I just discovered this site and this is my very first time commenting, but I love it. Keep up the great work. I guess what I did right this year was move my studio into my house. I had to do it for financial reasons at first. It ended up being such a great move though because it really helped me focus. Nobody is home all day since my kids are in school and my wife works outside our home. Being at home but being at work at the same time gave me a real sense of “why” I do this. Yes it’s fun to have a nice place that you can bring clients to and decorate like a man cave, but I don’t work with a team or any assistant. It’s just me. So the stoic walls and stillness of the place was getting to me and I didn’t know how much until I moved everything back home into our downstairs rec room. I didn’t know how much it was just sort of bringing me down. Now I can see my kids a few times a day on breaks when they get home from school and its given me a real freedom in my creative flow too. I’m writing better music and I even find that I communicate with people more effectively for some reason. Maybe its the comfort of my surroundings. Since its the only really big change I made this year I have to believe it has something to do with it.
