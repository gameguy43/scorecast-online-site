Weekend Provocation: I Dare You

Time for something different here on Weekend Provocation: I'm going to give you a few thousand dollars' worth of my time this weekend, in the form of a dare.

I defy any reader of SCOREcast Online to submit a term, in the comments, that I can't make relevant in some way to film scoring.

Leave your entry in the comments; I'll do my part by replying with the connection (as quickly as I can: if there's a deluge of comments—and I hope there will be!—it'll take me a little while to summon the cleverness to reply to them all).

Your term can be any (G-rated, please) word or phrase. For example:
Your term: Trafalgar Square.
My response: Trafalgar Square, located in central London, is a public forum for the display of art, just as film scoring is the display of our art (film music) in a public forum (the film itself). It is an intersection point—of major London thoroughfares—just as film composing is the intersection of many disparate elements—music, storytelling, technology, etc. Like the best film music, Trafalgar Square is easily accessible to anyone—a reminder that making your music connect to the audience requires a similarly clear point of access.

Like our composing rigs, Trafalgar Square is in a constant state of re-invention to integrate the latest technology (the most recent work to Trafalgar Square being a pump system and LED lighting, installed in May 2009).

Finally, Trafalgar Square, like the career of film music, must contend with the ongoing issue of congestion: a multitude of people trying to get in, with room for only so many at any given time. :-)


I'm betting you won't be able to stump me. Maybe in the future we'll do this contest with voting and prizes and all of that; for now, though, it's simply respect that's at stake. (What am I getting into here?!)

The cut-off time for this challenge is Sunday at 11:59 P.M., Los Angeles time (i.e., submissions arriving on Monday morning won't be answered).

So get busy, SCOREcasters—hit me with your best shot. I dare you.

Posted by Lee Sanders

SCOREcast is the leading online destination for the film music professional. Founded in 2006, and co-hosted by composers Deane Ogden and Lee Sanders, the one-two-punch combination of the SCOREcast Podcast and SCOREcastOnline.com now reaches over 11,500 listeners and readers worldwide and offers a view inside the world of composing for film via those who do it every day.

Open to both veteran and aspiring film composers, orchestrators, music editors, copyists, players, contractors, engineers, and assistants, SCOREcastOnline.com provides a great place to interact, share, and learn from people at all levels in the film music industry.
