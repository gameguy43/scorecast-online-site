Lee Sanders: Monday Morning… or Is It?

by Lee Sanders · View Comments
Share

Happy Monday, SCOREcasters—I’m powering up my studio as I write this, and it occurred to me that today is one of those rare Mondays (for me, at least) where it feels like the beginning of a work week.

Deadlines being what they are in this biz, our lives are frequently dissociated from the kind of rigid work schedule that most non-entrepreneurial sorts trudge through week after identical week.
For me, that’s always been an attractive part of the career. I set my own hours (which are often brutal—!), and that means I can eat when I want… go shopping when I want… sleep when I want.
To me, that’s liberating. But it’s also dangerous—right? It requires a kind of discipline. Every moment we dedicate to this career is a conscious choice. Sometimes it’s tough to stick by that choice, especially in the face of legitimate priorities like family and finances.
So it’s a two-part question today: 1) do you try to make your music schedule fit in with the rest of the working world—i.e., are you a 9-5 Monday-Friday composer? and 2) Do you think this aspect of our career is an advantage, a disadvantage, or simply a fact of life?
Take a couple of minutes, if your schedule will allow it, and let me know what you think.
