]]> By: Deane Ogden ../#comment-1294 Wed, 10 Mar 2010 01:05:00 +0000 ../../../../../?p=270#comment-1294 This is the third time someone has used the term “backlash”, and I’m not sure I’d go as far as to say that this is that. I would characterize more of what I witnessed as the kind of talk that you would hear at your second grader’s recess playground. I alluded to some of them in my opening statements:

“The Academy made a mistake”. “He doesn’t deserve it”. “UP sucked and it wasn’t the best score of the year.” “It was a rip-off of traditional French music.”

Some of these opinions *are* subjective, I agree… and therefore interesting to hear inasmuch as they are relevant. But other parts of them are nothing more than epithets that are uninformed, unfounded and rooted in childish meanness or jealousies left unaddressed. For example, I think we can all agree that while some people’s tastes would have led them to vote for “SHERLOCK HOLMES”, “UP!” certainly does *not* “suck”. Neither do the rest of the nominees, for that matter. They were all deserving.
