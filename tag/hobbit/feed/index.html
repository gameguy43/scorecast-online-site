Deane and Brian return from a two month break to chat with acclaimed Hollywood composer and orchestrator Conrad Pope about his life in music, his long-standing working relationship with John Williams, and his experience on this summer’s monster hit, Godzilla. Conrad also offers his thoughts on the Los Angeles musicians union saga, his first ever film score job, The Rocketeer, and how MIDI can positively or negatively affect a film score. Deane and Brian also squeeze in a discussion on the very prickly subject of the value of your reputation as a private person versus your reputation as a professional entity.

In the first episode of 2014, Deane and Brian sit down with orchestrator, music programmer and overall technical guru James Sizemore, who has been Howard Shore’s right-hand man since 2008. The boys delve into how James and Shore worked with visionary director Peter Jackson to bring the Hobbit films to the screen and what goes into collaborating on a cultural phenomenon that, when finished, will occupy the better part of five years for all involved. Additionally, the guys call BS on an article that appeared in The Guardian wherein a claim was made that “synths are killing” the film industry, as well as a discussion of a roundtable which focussed on having a “voice” as a composer and dealing with difficult directors.
